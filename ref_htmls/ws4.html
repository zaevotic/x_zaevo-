mind how_i_think.md
systems

Abstractions should earn their keep

Every layer of abstraction costs something — latency, debuggability, mental model space. I only reach for one when the complexity it hides is genuinely worse than the complexity it introduces.

tooling

The tool should disappear

Good tooling is invisible. You notice it only when it's gone. That's what I aim for — interfaces that feel like an extension of thought rather than a layer in between.

learning

Build the thing, read the paper later

I learn best when there's something broken in front of me. Reading without a problem to anchor it is expensive context with nowhere to go. I prefer to collide with a concept first.

design

Aesthetic and function are not at odds

A terminal that looks right is easier to work in. Taste is a forcing function — it makes you care enough to get the detail right.
